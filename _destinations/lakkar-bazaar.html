Lakkar Bazaar Shimla is a bustling market known primarily for its exquisite wooden handicrafts, locally crafted souvenirs, and vibrant street life. Located just below the famous Mall Road and adjacent to The Ridge, this market is a must-visit destination for tourists exploring Shimla. The name "Lakkar" means wood in Hindi, reflecting the bazaar’s specialty in wooden goods ranging from walking sticks to decorative items.

Historical Roots of Lakkar Bazaar

Lakkar Bazaar has its origins in the British colonial period when Shimla was the summer capital of British India. During this era, a group of skilled Sikh carpenters from Hoshiarpur settled in the area and started crafting and selling wooden furniture and utensils to meet the demand of the British officials and residents. Over time, the humble market evolved into a renowned hub for wooden handmade crafts made from local woods such as Shisham and apricot.

The bazaar’s legacy is a testament to the craftsmanship and cultural traditions of Himachal Pradesh, blending history with vibrant local artistry that continues to thrive today.

What Makes Lakkar Bazaar Unique?

This market is famous for its wide variety of wooden items including walking sticks, keychains, kitchen utensils, decorative candle stands, and intricate souvenir pieces. The craftsmanship reflects traditional Himachali styles, often polished and carved with intricate patterns that highlight the artisans' skills.

Besides wooden crafts, Lakkar Bazaar also offers Himachali woollens like shawls, caps, and gloves, as well as artificial jewelry and local souvenirs that capture the essence of Shimla's culture. The market atmosphere is lively, with narrow lanes lined by quaint wooden shops and bustling crowds creating an authentic hill station shopping experience.

Shopping Tips and Experience

  • Bargain Wisely: Haggling is common and encouraged; visitors can often get good deals on quality items.
  • Explore Fully: The bazaar is large and filled with hidden treasures, so plan to spend a few hours to browse extensively.
  • Local Flavors: Don’t miss the local street food stalls offering delicious momos, chaat, corn on the cob, and Himachali snacks to fuel your shopping spree.
  • Customization: Many shops offer personalized carving services if you want unique gifts or souvenirs.
  • Comfortable Shoes: Wear comfortable footwear as the streets are narrow and often crowded with tourists.

Entertainment and Attractions Around Lakkar Bazaar

Lakkar Bazaar is not just a shopping destination. It also features an ice skating rink during winters, adding to the lively vibe of the area. Nearby, there is an old cinema hall and several eateries where visitors can unwind after shopping.

The proximity to The Ridge makes it easy for tourists to combine a cultural walk with shopping, offering beautiful views of Shimla’s town and the surrounding Himalayan hills.

Visitor Information

  • Location: Mall Road, Shimla, near The Ridge
  • Opening Hours: 9:00 AM to 7:00 PM daily
  • Entry Fee: Free
  • Best Time to Visit: Morning or late afternoon to avoid heavy crowds and enjoy pleasant weather
  • How to Reach: Easily accessible on foot from Mall Road or The Ridge; taxis and buses are also available to drop visitors nearby

Why Visit Lakkar Bazaar?

  • Cultural Insight: Experience the rich craftsmanship and traditional woodwork of Himachal Pradesh.
  • Unique Souvenirs: Find one-of-a-kind wooden gifts and local handicrafts.
  • Vibrant Atmosphere: Enjoy the lively market vibe complemented by local foods and entertainment.
  • Convenient Location: Located in the heart of Shimla for easy access during your city tour.
  • Family Friendly: Something for everyone, including the ice rink and small eateries.

Conclusion

Lakkar Bazaar Shimla offers an immersive experience into the world of Himalayan craftsmanship and local culture. Its historic roots, coupled with a vibrant marketplace selling exquisite wooden crafts and Himachali products, make it an essential stop for any visitor to Shimla. Whether shopping for souvenirs, tasting local street food, or simply soaking in the lively atmosphere, Lakkar Bazaar fulfills the traveler's quest for authentic cultural experiences amidst the stunning backdrop of the Himalayas.
